Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about Memo Protocol.

How does verification work?

Memo uses a "Self-Transfer" mechanism. Instead of connecting your wallet to a website, you send 1 $MEMO (or 0.000001 SOL) to yourself. Our bot watches the blockchain for this specific transaction to verify you own the wallet.

Is it safe?

Yes, 100%. We are non-custodial. We never ask for your private keys or seed phrase. Since you are sending tokens to your own address, your funds never leave your control.

Can I use multiple wallets?

Absolutely. You can link as many wallets as you want to your Telegram account. The bot will sum up your balances from all linked wallets to determine your access level.

What is the "Self-Transfer"?

It's a transaction where the sender and receiver are the same address. It costs a tiny fraction of a cent in gas fees but cryptographically proves you control that wallet address.

How does wallet linking work?

It works by sending on-chain messages. To link two wallets, you simply send a transaction from one to the other. This "Memo" creates an undeniable cryptographic link on the blockchain, proving you control both wallets. That is the core of the Memo Protocol: using the chain itself to verify your identity graph.
